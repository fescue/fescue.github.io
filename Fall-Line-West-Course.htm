Fall Line - West Course

Fall Line Golf Club West Course is designed by Oglivy Cocking Mead(OCM). The West Course is inspired by sandbelt courses in Melbourne, Australia. Distinctive sharp bunker edges cut into green with immediate transition from putting green to the bunker edge resembling Metropolitan Golf Club in Oakleigh South, Victoria.

Fall Line Golf Club is in Butler, Georgia. Fairways are TifGrand bermudagrass running firm and fast. Greens are planted in TifEagle bermudagrass. Use of centipede grass in areas around bunkers contrast fast running areas and provide a texture to the landscape. Pete Dye used centipede grassing at Long Cove Club on Hilton Head Island, South Carolina in 1981 with architects Bobby Weed and Tom Doak.

Play from the first tee is shared with the East Course. The West course teeground plays down the fairway on the left to a par 5 of 535 yards.

Hole 2 is a drivable par 4 of 335 yards. The greensite is an angular left to right green with a slight front to back tilt. Hole 12 is also a drivable par 4 hole with another dramatic green.

Hole 15 is a par 5 hole of 570 yards with an sandy area resembling Pine Valley Hell's Half Acre splitting the second shot from the second fairway run up to the green.
